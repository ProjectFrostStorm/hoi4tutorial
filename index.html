Introduction to Hearts of Iron IV

By ProjectFrostStorm

Before we begin, HOI4 is a <5GB, CPU heavy game and as such, can run on a wide variety of machines, including laptops (but do beware of heat up during gameplay). However, do be aware that a slowdown is still to be expected towards the lategame due to the sheer number of units handled by both players and the AI.

Some images in this guide are full resolution 1920x1080 images scaled down to fit on screen. If something is difficult to read, feel free to open it in a new tab and use your browser's built-in zoom function!

Feel free to read this tutorial while playing along in the game, but it is not a requirement!

Chapter 0 - Initial Setup

So, you've downloaded Hearts of Iron IV and are finally ready to begin your story of conquest!
Once you've launched hoi4 from Steam, you should be greeted by the Paradox Interactive Launcher. Much like the one for Stellaris, here you can enable and disable mods and DLCs for the game, as well as modify certain launch settings.
Feel free to ignore everything here and just hit play!

Depending on whether you installed hoi4 on a hard drive or SSD, hoi4 can take up to a few minutes to load. It is normal for it to take surprising long to load on a first boot, much like Stellaris.
Once you're in, you'll be greeted by the hoi4 main menu! Head on over to the Options tab before you start playing.

Here, feel free to set your settings to those that you prefer, such as toning down graphical settings. However, be aware of two things.
One, audio is important in hoi4, as many important notification will play a sound effect when they occur. Learning to recognize these alerts will greatly improve your ability in playing!
And two, ensure the following graphical options are enabled, as it is easier to play with these visual indicators.

Next, head on over to the Multiplayer tab. Here is where you can host or join multiplayer games of hoi4.
Once you're here, make sure to change your nickname on the top left where it says player! All players are given this nickname by default, so it'll be confusing if we have a bunch of "Players" running around in our games.
Here, it is most reliable to either join games via Steam or have the host send you the server ID and use the Connect to Server ID button.

Here's what the multiplayer lobby looks like. Besides the inclusion of the multiplayer lobby, it is almost identical to what it looks like in singleplayer.

Head back to the main menu and go to the Singleplayer tab. Let's actually jump into the game now!
Make sure to select the 1936 Gathering Storm scenario.

You'll now find yourself on the Interesting Countries screen. Here, you can select one of the major countries (known as majors) on the top or one of the minor countries (known as minors) that have additional content on the bottom. Feel free to look through each country to see what interests you!
Now, hit the Other countries option to see the potential that hoi4 can offer.

And here's something that truly makes hoi4 special. Any nation present on the map, you can play as by using LMB (left clicking)!
To navigate the map, you can either use the arrow keys or click and drag using MMB (the mouse wheel).
Scroll up and down to zoom in and out of the map.

Once you feel accustomed to the controls of map navigation (you'll need to do this alot in the game!) feel free to pick a nation and get started. I recommend picking a major to begin with, specifically the German Reich or Italy (be careful that Italy starts in a war with Ethiopia!).
Make sure to enable historical AI focuses so you can rely on your knowledge of history!

And with that, you're starting your first game of Hearts of Iron IV! This marks the end of chapter 0 of this tutorial.
Now, we'll really start getting into the game with the next chapter!

Chapter 1 - Beginner Section

1.0 - General Tips

Before we start this section, I'd like to begin with some small tips that you should keep in mind throughout this entire guide.

  1. Many things have tooltips! When in doubt, mouse over something. Chances are, it'll pop up a tooltip explaining more details!
  2. Hoi4 takes advantages of colours alot. If something is green, that's generally a good thing. If something is red, that's generally bad. Even if you don't know what something does, you can use this rule of thumb to figure out if it's beneficial or not!

And with that, let's jump into things! As we've left off in the previous chapter, you should now be in a game of hoi4. Don't be overwhelmed by the number of buttons! We'll slowly, but surely, get through all of them.
For now, turn your attention to the alerts near the top of the screen.

These alerts generally indicate important issues plaguing your country that you should address. Mousing over them will give additional details on what the issue at hand is.
Left click them to be sent to the relevant menu to address the issue and right click to dismiss them (but don't do any of that now!).

These issue cover a wide range of gameplay, so we'll tackle them slowly and in a particular order. Hopefully this guide will explain everything clearly!

1.1 - Basic Country Management

1.1.1 - National Focus

First, turn your attention to this icon.

This indicates that a National Focus has not been set. You can access the National Focus Tree via the Political Tab which you can open by clicking your flag or by pressing Q.

Here, you'll be brought to your country's national focus tree. These trees are different between countries to reflect different situations different countries found themselves in the 1930s. Countries that do not have content (or is locked behind DLC) will use the Generic Focus Tree instead.
To naviagate the tree, click and drag (LMB) to move around the tree and scroll up and down to zoom in and out.

To view a focus, left click it to see its requirements, effects, and time to complete as well as the option to start completing it.

Focuses that are gold are completed. Ones that are grey are available. And ones that are black are still locked.

Like a proper tree, the focus tree has branches and exclusive paths. Sometimes the lines can be confusing, so just read the focus requirements!

Many focuses typically take 70 days to complete and only one focus can be completed at any one time. You'll generally be able to get through all focuses within a game, but keep in mind that some focuses might no longer be worth it towards the end!
If you're playing along as the German Reich or Italy, I recommend starting with Rhineland or Ethiopian War Logistics as your first focus.

Note that HOI4 runs at one hour per tick in contrast to Stellaris which runs at one day per tick. To compensate, while Stellaris runs from 2200 to 2500, HOI4 runs from 1936 to 1948.

1.1.2 - Research

Now we can move on to the second tree system in HOI4, the research tree.
Turn your attention to this icon.

This indicates that you have research slots not currently in use. You can access the Research Tab by clicking the little magnifying glass and beaker icon or by pressing W.

Clicking any of these slots will bring you to the research tree. Unlike national focus trees, research trees are the same for every country, though different countries may start with different technologies.
Technologies in green are already researched. Those in yellow are available and those in grey are still locked.

To naviagate the tree, click and drag (LMB) to move around. Use left click to select technologies and change tabs.

Unlike national focus trees, research trees are split into different tabs which can be access along the top. These tabs are colour coded based on what field they fall in, with green meaning land units, blue meaning navy, red meaning airforce, and orange meaning civilian.
To simplify things, feel free to ignore most research tabs and focus only on the following.

You may have already noticed the years on the screen that roughly line up with certain technologies. This indicates what year a technology is from and researching technologies that are in the future will encur a time ahead penalty. This penalty lessens the closer you get to the actual time of the technology.
Only Doctrine technologies (in their respective doctrine tabs) are timeless.

Multiple research slots work independent of each other, so make sure all your research slots are always researching something!
I recommending starting with Basic Machine Tools or Concentrated Industry I, Construction I, Electronic Mechanical Engineering or Mechanical Computing, and either Support Weapons I or the next Land Doctrine technology as your first technologies.

Lastly, note that not all technologies give existing things bonuses. Some, instead, introduce brand new equipment that need to be produced before you get its benefit. What do I mean about producing equipment? Well keep that in the back of your mind because it'll come up in a little bit!

And that's the end of the trees! This may be a good time to take a break and let what you've learnt sink in. Or to maybe reread everything up till now to really cement that understanding. Either way, good work! That's two major game concepts out of the way for now!

1.1.3 - Construction

Now let's move on to buildings and construction. Turn your attention to this icon.

This indicates that you have Civilian Factories (often shortened to civs) that are standing idle.
Civs represent the civilian industrial capabilities of your country and thus tend to make up the backbone of your economy. Buildings and structures in hoi4 are built by civs.
To put them to work, head over to the Construction Tab by clicking the little digger or by pressing T.

The construction tab is where you can queue up buildings to be built, but before we talk about how to do so, we need to talk about states and provinces.

When you click a piece of territory on land, two things will be highlighted: the smaller province in bright yellow and the larger state in white.
Also open is some information about the state and province. Note that only states have names (this state is New York).

Provinces are the smallest subdivision of the hoi4 map and is the grid system for ground units, meaning ground units move and attack along province lines (a province can only have units from one allied side within it during wartime!).
States are collections of provinces and is the smallest subdivision of territory in terms of control (i.e. in peace time, a state can only be controlled by exactly one country). Claims and Cores are thus also enacted at the state level.

Why this is important, is because buildings in hoi4 come in three flavours: state structures (top), shared structures (middle), and province structures (bottom).
The most important buildings and structures are state and shared structures, both types of which are built at the state level.

We'll be ignoring most of the state structures for now, but let's take a quick peek at how building them works. Select the infastructure railway icon in the construction tab.

Whoa! That's a lot of stuff! Don't worry about most of it, what is important is that each state has a number out of ten at the top. This number indicates the current level of infastructure in that state, which can go up to a maximum of ten.
All state structures work the same way, where the structure can be upgraded to its maximum, no matter the state (as long as you own it).
Also note that all these states are in green. States that you can still build in will be green, while states that are full will be blue.

Let's avoid building any infastructure for now. You can exit this mode by exiting the construction tab or you can select a different building.

Let's start actually building! We should begin with the two most important buildings of hoi4, civilian factories and military factories. A country needs its industry for both its economy and military, so let's queue up some factories!
Select the orange civilian factory icon in the construction tab.

Another build map! This time it should be less intimidating. And look, some states are now in blue, indicating that they are full for civilian factories!
You've probably now noticed that this time, the build cap is different for each state. This is because, unlike infastructure, civilian factories are shared buildings.

Each state (depending on how populated and industrious it is) has a different number of shared building slots. These slots are shared among all shared buildings.
For example, New York has 12 starting building slots, so only 12 shared buildings can be built in that state.

The number of building slots a state has can be increased via some national focuses (and typically with some free buildings) and through the concentrated industry or dispersed industry technology, up to a maximum of 25 building slots.

Now, let's actually start building!
To build in a state, left click to add it to the queue, right click to remove it from the queue.
Shift left click fills the state with the building you selected, shift right click empties the state of the building queues.
Control left click places the order to the top of the queue.
States with yellow stripes have your currently selected building in the queue, states with blue stripes have your currently selected building in the queue and have no more free building slots.

Now with your factory orders in the queue, your civilian factories will work to complete those orders. Up to 15 civs can work on the same order at the same time. The little progress bar can be moused over to see more details, such as when it'll finish building.
Extra civs (beyond the 15 working on the initial order) will work on subsequent orders, but only on subsequent orders of different buildings or in different states (e.g. the extra factories in the image are working on Ohio's next factory, not Florida's second dockyard).
You can change the order of the queue either by click and dragging them or by using the arrows. Shift left clicking the arrows will send them to the beginning or end of the queue. Progress on a build order will be saved if you change the queue order.
You can use the little plus and minus buttons to add or remove building queues (shift clicking the plus button also fills the state). The X button will cancel all of that one type of queue (with a confirmation).

Whew, that was a lot! Now's a good time to take a break or try a bit of building yourself. Make sure that you feel comfortable with how construction works if you're currently trying it out in a game.
I suggest your first constructions as a major to be civs in states with the highest infastructure bonus (that little green percentage when you're on the factory build map).

Before we move on, if you're keen-eyed, you may have notice a discrepancy in the build numbers. For example, in the above image, it says that I have 165/165 civs, but yet only 32 are actually working on building stuff. This is because all remaining 133 are being used for consumer goods.
Hoi4 is a game about war and not about peacetime (if you're interested in something with more peacetime focus, try Victoria II or Victoria 3 when it comes out), so we don't care about what the needs of civilians are. As such, consumer goods give no benefits at all and only take up civilian factories that could've been used for building.
We'll be touching on internal politics later on, but for now keep in the back of your mind that things that decrease consumer goods factories are good and things that increase it are bad.

1.1.4 - Production

An army without guns is just a big crowd and it is up to you to plan your country's war effort! That's right, it's time to talk about equipment production!
Turn your attention now to this icon.

This indicates you have Military Factories (often shortened to mils) that are idle.
Unlike civilian factories, military factories are tooled to produce military hardware.
To add them to production lines, head over to the Production Tab by clicking the little wrench and gear or by pressing Y.

In hoi4, equipment are produced in assembly lines, where many factories can work together on producing one kind of equipment.
Let's focus on just one assembly line. The assembly line indicates what equipment it is producing, its rate of production, and how many mils are assigned to the production line.

More factories can be assigned using the little plus and minus buttons (holding down control changes it to increments of five, shift changes it to increements of ten) or by clicking a square in the grid of factories (clicking the x5 and x10 changes the increments).

Notice that in the top production line, 6 of the 13 factories are greyed out. This is because we've assigned more factories than there currently exists. When new mils are made available, they will automatically be assigned to these greyed out position, in priority from top to bottom.

A new production line can be created using the buttons at the top of the tab. They are sorted by equipment type, being split between infantry, armor, and air. Simply select what equipment you wish to produce and a new production line will be created at the bottom.
Productions lines can be moved around by clicking and dragging them and can be deleted by clicking the little trash icon (with a confirmation).

You can also change what equipment an existing production line is producing, which is typically only used for changing production to an upgraded equipment. To do so, simply click the equipment picture and find the replacing equipment (be careful to select the right equipment for reasons coming up!).
Outdated equipment being produced will have a brown background and you'll also get an alert icon.

The last two things we'll be covering about production lines are production efficiency and resources.
Starting with production efficiency, it is why you should be careful about switching equipment. Essentially, the longer a production line is left untouched, the better it becomes at producing equipment. This bonus is reduced when adding new factories and when switching production.

Next, all equipment requires resources to produce. A unit of resource in hoi4 is not a single unit of resource, but rather a constant income of a unit of resource. Resources will be covered later on, but for now, know that a production line that is missing resources will produce equipment and earn efficiency at a slower rate (or even 0 depending on the equipment and missing resources!).

You'll notice that we've only been dealing with the green material in the production tab and not the blue material. Similar to ground and air equipment, ships are produced with dockyards, but have some key differences. Due to this, don't worry about it for now!
If you do have extra dockyards sitting around, feel free to put them on to convoys.

And with that, you've made it past the production mechanics! This is a good time to stop and take in everything, as production is one of the most densely packed gameplay feature. Feel free to take some time and familiarize yourself with production lines when you get a chance.
If you're following along in game, I suggest starting your production line in a similar fashion to mine below.

1.1.5 - Government

That was a lot of difficult gameplay mechanics! We'll finish up this section with a small intro to managing your government.
Navigate back to the politics tab by clicking your flag or pressing Q.

At the top of the screen, there are three stats/resources for your nation. From left to right, they are political power, stability, and war support.
Below the national focus button, you have your country's national spirits.
The final thing we'll discuss in this section is your governing laws and advisors.

First, the three at the top. Political power is passively earned per day at a base rate of 2 per day, but 1 is also consumed each day a national focus is progressing. It is used to modify your government and to enact decisions. Political power can go into the negatives and is capped at 2000.
Next, stability and war support are measures of your population's opinion of your country. They range between 0% to 100%, but the value can go over and under this cap (negative values will be displayed, but values over 100 will not). The base line value for both is 50%, where you recieve no positive nor negative effects. You can mouse over both values to see what benefits or penalties are being applied.

National spirits are representation of forces acting within your country. They can have positive, negative, or a mixed of both effects. They are typically gained or removed through national focuses or events.

Laws, advisors, designers, and military staff of your government can be changed at any time, given you have the political power and meet the prerequisites for whatever you are changing. They give various useful buffs to your country.

And that's it! Short one this time, as learning about laws and government is best done through experience. It doesn't matter too much if you hire incorrect advisors, so feel free to try things out!
With that, we've completed the basic country management section! In our next section, we'll be learning about basic army management. Feel free to take a break and come back later!
